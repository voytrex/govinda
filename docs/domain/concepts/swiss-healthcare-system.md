# Swiss Healthcare System Overview

## Introduction

Switzerland has a unique healthcare system that combines mandatory basic insurance with optional supplementary coverage. Unlike many countries with single-payer systems, Switzerland uses a **regulated private insurance model** where multiple competing insurers offer standardized basic coverage.

> **Key Principle**: Every person residing in Switzerland must have basic health insurance (Grundversicherung). The coverage is identical across all insurers - only premiums and service quality differ.

## Key Stakeholders

### 1. Insured Persons (Versicherte)

Every person living in Switzerland must:
- Obtain basic insurance within **3 months** of taking residence
- Pay monthly premiums directly to their insurer
- Share costs through franchise and co-payment (Selbstbehalt)

### 2. Health Insurers (Krankenversicherer)

Approved insurers must:
- Accept all applicants for basic insurance (no risk selection)
- Offer identical coverage as defined by KVG
- Submit premiums annually for BAG approval
- Maintain required reserves

> **As of 2025**: ~50 health insurers operate in Switzerland

### 3. Healthcare Providers (Leistungserbringer)

- Doctors and specialists
- Hospitals and clinics
- Pharmacies
- Therapists and other providers

### Key Dates

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **Late September** | BAG approves premiums for next year |
| **Early October** | Insurers publish new premiums |
| **30 November** | Deadline to terminate and switch insurers (ordinary) |
| **1 January** | New insurance year begins |

### Extraordinary Termination

Insured persons may also switch mid-year in these cases:
- Move to a different premium region
- Premium increase announcement (special termination right)
- After certain life events

## Coverage Overview

### What Basic Insurance (KVG) Covers

âœ… **Included**:
- Doctor visits and specialist consultations
- Hospital stays (general ward)
- Medications on the specialties list (SpezialitÃ¤tenliste)
- Laboratory tests
- Physiotherapy (with prescription)
- Maternity care and childbirth
- Emergency treatment abroad (up to 2x Swiss rate)
- Preventive examinations (certain screenings)

âŒ **Not Included**:
- Dental care (except accidents)
- Private/semi-private hospital rooms
- Alternative medicine (limited)
- Glasses and contact lenses (adults)
- Cosmetic procedures

### What Supplementary Insurance (VVG) Can Add

Common supplementary products:
- **Hospital supplementary** (Spitalzusatzversicherung): Private/semi-private rooms
- **Dental** (Zahnversicherung): Dental treatments
- **Alternative medicine** (Alternativmedizin): Complementary therapies
- **Travel insurance** (Auslandversicherung): Extended coverage abroad
- **Daily allowance** (Taggeldversicherung): Income protection during illness

## Cost Sharing Model

When you incur medical costs, you pay:

### 1. Franchise (Annual Deductible)

- You pay 100% of costs until your franchise is reached
- Resets every calendar year
- Higher franchise = lower monthly premium
- See [Franchise System](./franchise-system.md) for details

### 2. Selbstbehalt (Co-Payment)

- After franchise: you pay 10% of remaining costs
- Maximum per year:
  - Adults: CHF 700
  - Children: CHF 350
- Example: CHF 7,000 in costs after franchise = CHF 700 co-payment

### 3. Insurance Coverage

- Insurer pays remaining costs (90% after franchise, then 100%)

### Federal Laws

| Law | Abbreviation | Purpose |
|-----|--------------|---------|

| Body | Role |
|------|------|

| **FINMA** | Financial supervision of insurers |
